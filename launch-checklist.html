Vestra Launch Checklist

Everything required to take Vestra (Kenya-first proptech) from code to live paying users — legalities, licenses, costs, timeline. Prepared 2026-07-04.

Bottom line. Minimum viable launch requires ~KES 8.9M (~USD 68,500) upfront plus ~KES 1.3M/mo (~USD 10,000) recurring across legal, compliance, banking, infrastructure, engineering, operations, and marketing. Critical path is 4-6 months, bounded by partner-bank trust account (3-6 months) and ODPC registration (2-4 weeks) — both hard legal walls. Everything else can parallelize.

1. Executive summary

Vestra targets the transactional layer of Kenyan real estate — verified escrow, KRA-compliant rent, agent trust graph — a moat none of Property24 KE, BuyRentKenya, HassConsult, Optiven, or Username currently occupies. Business launch requires eleven categories of parallel work: five with hard legal gates (company registration, ODPC, partner-bank trust, KRA tax, insurance) and six that can compress with capital (engineering, ops, content, marketing, fundraising, integrations).

Assumptions in this document: (a) founding team is 2-3 Kenyan citizens; (b) starting capital ≥ KES 10M; (c) launch scope is Nairobi + Kiambu counties, rent + sale + escrow verticals, no cross-border, no credit (defer DCP licence to Phase 2). Diaspora vertical launches month 9.

2. Company formation & corporate

2.1 Private limited company registration

  • Register a Private Limited Company via eCitizen BRS portal. Requirements: proposed name (3 alternatives), memorandum + articles of association (BRS templates OK), registered office address, minimum 1 director, minimum 1 shareholder. Directors must have KRA PINs.
  • Choose objects broadly — "provision of technology platforms for real-estate transactions, escrow services, digital rental management, related property services".
  • Beneficial-ownership disclosure filed at incorporation (Companies (Beneficial Ownership) Regulations 2020).

Cost: KES 10,650 (name reservation + registration) + advocate drafting if not using templates KES 25,000-75,000.

Timeline: 5-14 working days.

2.2 Cap table + shareholders' agreement

  • Shareholders' agreement (SHA) covering vesting, drag/tag, ROFR, board composition, reserved matters — non-negotiable before first external hire or investor. Draft with corporate counsel.
  • Employee Share Option Plan (ESOP) trust set up before first hire — typically 10-15% pool.

Cost: SHA drafting KES 150,000-350,000. ESOP setup KES 100,000-250,000.

2.3 Business permit (Nairobi County)

  • Single Business Permit at ward level from Nairobi City County (or the county of registered office). Category = "Professional / Consultancy / Technology".

Cost: KES 15,000-50,000/yr depending on office size + ward.

Renewal: annual.

2.4 Registered office + physical address

  • Registered office required at incorporation. Coworking space (Nairobi Garage, iHub, Workstyle) satisfies this — approximately KES 40,000-90,000/mo for 3-4 desks + registered address.

3. Data protection (ODPC + DPO)

Under the Data Protection Act 2019 plus Data Protection (General) Regulations 2021, Vestra is a Data Controller AND Processor from day one — financial services + sensitive data (national IDs, KRA PINs, bank details) both trigger registration.

3.1 ODPC registration

  • Register as Data Controller AND Data Processor via odpc.go.ke portal.
  • Documents: KRA PIN certificate, incorporation certificate, DPIA summary, list of processing activities, third-party processors list.
  • Fee tiers by turnover: micro/small KES 4,000, medium KES 20,000, large KES 40,000. Vestra starts at micro/small.

Cost: KES 4,000-8,000 (both roles).

Timeline: 2-6 weeks.

Renewal: every 24 months.

3.2 Data Protection Officer (DPO)

  • Financial-services + systematic-monitoring-at-scale designation makes DPO mandatory, not optional.
  • Options: (a) fractional DPO service (KES 150,000-300,000/mo), (b) in-house senior privacy counsel (KES 500,000-1,000,000/mo). Recommend fractional for first 12 months.

3.3 Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)

  • Required before processing begins for high-risk categories: KYC biometric, financial transactions, tenant scoring. Standard 15-30 page document per activity.

Cost: KES 150,000-400,000 (one-time, with DPO or counsel).

3.4 Privacy operations

  • 72-hour breach notification runbook — incident detection, ODPC portal submission form, subject-notification templates.
  • Subject Access Request (SAR) response workflow — 30-day statutory response.
  • Records of Processing Activities (ROPA) register — living document.
  • Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) signed with every processor: Smile Identity, IntaSend, Flutterwave, MapTiler, Cohere, Deepgram, Anthropic, Sentry, PostHog, Grafana, Cloudflare, Bunny.net.
Enforcement risk. ODPC has issued fines: Roma School KES 4.55M, Mulla Pride KES 2.975M (upheld by High Court), Casa Vera KES 1.85M. Amendment Bill 2025 proposes cap change from "whichever lower" to "whichever higher" — potentially unlimited 1%-of-turnover fines.

4. Banking & payment licences

4.1 Business bank account

  • Operating account at a Tier-1 KE bank (Equity, KCB, Co-op, NCBA). Not the same institution as the trust-account partner (segregation).

Cost: KES 0-5,000 setup; monthly maintenance KES 500-2,000.

4.2 Partner-bank trust account (BLOCKING)

Critical: Under the National Payment System Act 2011, holding client funds without a bank-operated trust account is a criminal offence for unlicensed entities. This is the single hardest blocker to Vestra's escrow product.

  • Vestra rides on a commercial bank's trust operations. Bank holds trust account in its own name; Vestra is the instruction-giving party under a trust deed.
  • Recommend HFC first (diaspora infrastructure overlap) or NCBA (best API DX post-ConnectPlus launch Oct 2025). Fallback Equity (Jenga API) or Co-op (SACCO/chama-adjacent).
  • Requires: incorporation docs, audited financials (or letter of undertaking for new co), fit-and-proper directors, DPO evidence, KYC/AML program document, escrow flow diagram, indemnity + insurance evidence.

Cost: Trust deed drafting KES 400,000-900,000 (both sides' counsel). Bank account setup fee KES 25,000-100,000. Ongoing custodian fee 0.1-0.35% of average balance monthly.

Timeline: 3-6 months. Start immediately.

4.3 Safaricom Daraja (M-Pesa) production credentials

  • Apply via Safaricom Business Portal for production shortcode. Sandbox is free; production requires signed Merchant Agreement.
  • Separate C2B shortcode (rent collection) and B2C shortcode (landlord payouts) — cannot combine on same shortcode.
  • Callbacks must be publicly reachable HTTPS, idempotent, and return HTTP 200 always (Daraja retries).

Cost: Setup KES 50,000-100,000 per shortcode. Monthly maintenance KES 3,000-8,000 each. Transaction fees: Paybill 1.5%, Buy Goods 0.5%, B2C flat KES 30-70 per payout.

Timeline: 4-8 weeks.

4.4 Aggregator payments (IntaSend + Flutterwave)

  • Both KES-native, self-service onboarding, no Safaricom Merchant Agreement needed. Use as Daraja fallback + card + Airtel Money coverage.

Cost: IntaSend 3.5% STK / 1.5% Buy Goods / PesaLink 0.5% + KES 20. Flutterwave 3.8% cards. Onboarding KES 0.

Timeline: 3-10 days.

4.5 CBK NPS Division comfort letter

  • Non-binding but strongly recommended: engage CBK NPS Division for a comfort letter confirming the rent-collection pass-through does not require PSP licence.

Cost: Regulatory counsel KES 200,000-500,000.

Do NOT do in Phase 1: PSP licence (KES 5M-50M capital), e-money issuer licence, DCP / NDTCP credit licence. Defer to Phase 2 via separate SPV (Vestra Credit Ltd) if you build Rent Advance.

5. Communications compliance (CAK)

5.1 CAK Sender ID + SMS aggregator onboarding

  • Register Sender ID "VESTRA" via CAK-licensed aggregator (Africa's Talking, Celcom, Bongo Live, Onfon). Aggregator handles CAK submission.
  • Separate transactional (rent receipts, tax alerts — any-time OK) from promotional (07:00-19:00 window, DND-compliant, opt-in) message classes in code.

Cost: Sender ID KES 5,000-15,000 one-time. SMS unit price KES 0.30-0.80 per message. Monthly minimum KES 5,000-20,000.

Timeline: 5-15 days.

5.2 Email domain + transactional email

  • Provider: Postmark ($15/mo starter) or Amazon SES ($0.10 per 1,000). Set up SPF + DKIM + DMARC. Domain warm-up 2-3 weeks.

Cost: USD 15-60/mo.

5.3 WhatsApp Business API (deferred but plan for it)

  • Kenya has 40M+ WhatsApp users; 24-hour free-reply window inside a customer-initiated conversation. Route via 360dialog, Twilio, or MessageBird.

Cost: Business Solution Provider fee USD 40-100/mo + Meta conversation fees (Kenya utility ~USD 0.006, marketing ~USD 0.04). Verification 5-10 days.

6. Tax registration & ongoing filing

6.1 KRA registrations at incorporation

  • Corporate KRA PIN — free via iTax.
  • VAT registration — mandatory above KES 5M turnover; voluntary below. Recommend voluntary early to reclaim input VAT.
  • PAYE — required from first employee.
  • Turnover Tax (TOT) — for turnover KES 1M-25M/yr, 3% of gross. May apply pre-scale-up.

Cost: KES 0 (self-service). Ongoing accountant retainer KES 25,000-80,000/mo.

6.2 Statutory employee contributions

  • NSSF (National Social Security Fund) — up to KES 4,320/employee/mo (Tier I + II combined, 2026 rate) split employer/employee.
  • SHIF (Social Health Insurance Fund, replaced NHIF Oct 2024) — 2.75% of gross salary.
  • Housing Levy — 1.5% employer + 1.5% employee of gross salary.
  • Affordable Housing Levy — collected via PAYE.

6.3 eTIMS onboarding

  • Mandatory for all Vestra invoices from Jan 2026. Onboard via KRA eTIMS portal or through a KRA-approved eTIMS OSP (Online Service Provider). If Vestra resells eTIMS to landlord clients, register as OSP.

Cost: KES 0 direct; OSP fees KES 500-2,000 per issuing party per month if reselling.

6.4 eRITS integration (Vestra product feature)

  • Integrate Gava Connect API to file landlord MRI returns. Register as KRA integrated party (developer + production certificates).

Cost: KES 0 direct; engineering effort ~3-4 weeks.

7. Sector-specific licences

7.1 Estate Agents Registration Board (EARB)

  • Vestra as a platform is not an estate agent — no EARB registration needed if we don't hold ourselves out as agents.
  • Individual EARB-licensed agents onboard onto Vestra with their existing licence.
  • Cache the public EARB Members Directory for verification badges. ~500 licensed agents nationally.

7.2 Advocate on retainer

  • Title verification, POA drafting, dispute handling. Not a licence — a service.

Cost: Retainer KES 80,000-200,000/mo + per-matter billing.

7.3 Land Control Board consent (workflow, not licence)

  • For agricultural-land sales outside municipalities, LCB consent mandatory. Application digitized via Ardhisasa but hearings still monthly per sub-county.

8. Consumer-facing legal documents

All required before first live user. Draft with counsel, publish before launch.

8.1 Terms of Service

  • Platform ToS with clear disclaimer that Vestra is not an estate agent + limitation of liability + dispute resolution (Kenya arbitration + High Court jurisdiction).

8.2 Privacy Policy

  • DPA-2019-compliant. Names all processors (see §3.4 DPA list). Cookie policy overlay.

8.3 Escrow Agreement template

  • Tri-party (buyer + seller + Vestra as instruction-giver on trust account) or bilateral variants for rent deposit. Signed via advanced e-signature (KICA §83J).

8.4 Landlord Services Agreement (rent management + KRA filing)

  • Covers rent collection, payout schedule, MRI remittance authority, fee schedule.

8.5 Tenancy Agreement template

  • Standard KE tenancy customized per Vestra deposit + move-out rules.

8.6 Refund Policy + Complaints Policy

8.7 Acceptable Use Policy (agents + listings)

8.8 Cookie Consent Notice

Total cost for full document suite: KES 300,000-700,000 with commercial counsel.

10. Intellectual property

10.1 Trademark "Vestra" + logo

  • File at KIPI (Kenya Industrial Property Institute). Classes 36 (financial / insurance / real estate) and 42 (software / technology). Consider Class 35 (advertising / business management) if selling agent SaaS.

Cost: KES 15,000 per class + KES 15,000 per class publication + agent fees KES 25,000-60,000 per class.

Timeline: 6-14 months to registration; use TM (unregistered) mark from day one.

10.2 Domain registration

  • vestra.co.ke via KENIC-accredited registrar (Truehost, Sasahost, HostAfrica). Grab vestra.com if available (currently third-party held — investigate cost). Add vestra.app, vestra.africa defensively.

Cost: .co.ke KES 1,300/yr; .com KES 1,500-5,000/yr (or 5-6-figure USD acquisition if squatted); .app USD 15/yr; .africa USD 30/yr.

10.3 Copyright register (code + Hass-equivalent index)

  • Register core software + property-index methodology at KECOBO (Kenya Copyright Board) once Vestimate has real data (month 12+).

Cost: KES 1,000 per registration.

20. Risks & contingencies

Legal / regulatory risks.
  • Bank refuses trust arrangement: pivot to a specialized fintech trust operator (rare in KE) OR structure as advocate-held client account for first 6 months while re-approaching another Tier-1 bank. Cost: legal-only escrow adds friction + advocate liability.
  • Finance Bill 2026 passes 10% MRI + non-resident 30% withholding at unfavourable dates: Vestra rate config already parameterized. Impact: landlord churn to informal channels; mitigate with content education campaign.
  • ODPC amendment expands fine caps to "whichever higher": maintain zero-breach posture; DPIA-first product development.
Product / operational risks.
  • Field verifier discovers fake title on live listing: tenant/buyer already paid — trigger refund from escrow within 24h; deregister the agent; publish incident to trust ledger.
  • Daraja outage during rent-payment window: failover to IntaSend + Flutterwave via `PaymentProvider` interface; retries via BullMQ.
  • Ardhisasa API becomes public but with breaking schema: abstract behind adapter; keep human-in-loop path as fallback for 6 months post-API.
  • Fraudulent developer runs polished listing set: gated by geofenced-photo verification + Ardhisasa cross-check + trust ledger from day one.
Market / competitive risks.
  • Property24 SA parent reallocates capital to KE: 18-24 month entrench window. Prioritize agent lock-in via role-split commissions and landlord lock-in via KRA MRI integration — both punitive to switch out of.
  • Spleet enters Kenya with rent-financing before Vestra: ship Rent Advance v1 by month 9 in Vestra Credit Ltd wrapper.
  • BuyRentKenya (Rushbox) turns around with fresh capital: keep momentum on agent BD and content SEO to hold post-acquisition confusion window.
Financial risks.
  • Runway underestimated: hire schedule can be deferred by 1-2 months; agent BD can compress with commission-heavy pay. Keep 3-month buffer minimum.
  • Escrow float loss: insured under fidelity guarantee + partner-bank indemnity; audit reconciliation daily.
