# Global Proptech Playbooks for Vestra (Kenya-first)

## 1. KE Holdings / Beike (China)

**Business model.** 2024 GTV RMB3.35T (~US$459B), net revenue RMB93.5B (+20.2% YoY). Revenue is split between existing-home transactions (agent commissions net of ACN split), new-home distribution (dev-paid commissions, higher take-rate), plus "home renovation, rental and emerging" services now above 30% of revenue. Not a listings-fee model — it monetises the transaction itself. [KE 2024 results](https://investors.ke.com/news-releases/news-release-details/ke-holdings-inc-announces-fourth-quarter-and-fiscal-year-2024)

**The wedge.** The **Agent Cooperation Network (ACN)** — the transaction is split into ~10 defined roles (lister, key-holder, tour-agent, closer, etc.), each earns a rules-based commission share. 70% of GTV now flows through ACN and 75% of active agents on the platform are *not* from Lianjia (Beike's captive brokerage). [Online Marketplaces on ACN](https://www.onlinemarketplaces.com/articles/ke-holdings-the-most-interesting-real-estate-company-in-the-world/)

**Trust.** Every listing verified against title records + "genuine listing" pledge (photos, price, availability, exclusivity all guaranteed). Agent reputation score determines job assignment and commission %. [Beike Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beike)

**Payments.** Escrow-like transaction pipe — buyer funds held while ownership transfer completes. Also runs mortgage origination.

**Agent network.** Neither open nor closed — a **cooperative-with-scoring** hybrid across 49,700 stores and 445,000 agents.

**Data moat.** "Housing Dictionary" — every unit in every building keyed, with photos, floorplans, transaction history. Impossible to replicate without door-to-door canvassing.

**Copy for KE.** The role-split commission engine is the single most important idea in global proptech for a fragmented market. Kenya has 50k semi-formal agents fighting over the same listing — pay each of them for their real contribution (lister vs. tour vs. closer) and you eliminate the "same house on 6 apps at 3 prices" problem.

**Don't copy.** Owning a captive brokerage (Lianjia). In KE the regulator (EARB) already exists and the capital cost is too high.

## 2. QuintoAndar (Brazil)

**Business model.** ~US$750M ARR at last disclosure; ~$5.1B valuation. Revenue = broker fee (~1 month's rent upfront) + 8–10% recurring landlord management fee + 5–6% sales commission when they broker purchases.

**The wedge.** **Killed the fiador (guarantor).** Brazilian rentals historically required a third-party property-owning guarantor or 3-month deposit. QuintoAndar underwrites the tenant credit risk itself and *guarantees the landlord's rent every month*. That single product wedge unlocked a market where >70% of urban Brazilians couldn't qualify to rent.

**Trust.** Digital signatures replaced notaries. Tenants screened via proprietary credit model. Contract, payment, damage-insurance all on-platform.

**Payments.** Full escrow: tenant pays QuintoAndar → QuintoAndar pays landlord on the 1st regardless of tenant default. Landlord damage insurance included.

**Agent network.** Originally direct (no agents). Later opened "Rede QuintoAndar" — SaaS for independent brokers. Acquired Grupo Navent in 2021 to become LatAm-wide portal.

**Data moat.** Tenant credit graph across LatAm — nobody else has payment history on 5M+ renters.

**Copy for KE.** The **guaranteed-rent product** is the single highest-leverage move for Kenya. Landlords in Nairobi demand 2–3 months deposit + guarantor because they can't trust tenant payment. Underwrite the risk, pay landlords on the 1st via M-Pesa, and you can charge them 10% forever.

**Don't copy.** The unlimited-guarantee product itself was so risky QuintoAndar **shut down QuintoCred and terminated 45,000 guarantee contracts in 2024**. Guarantee must be priced dynamically per-tenant, not flat.

## 3. NoBroker (India)

**Business model.** FY24 operating revenue ₹803 Cr (+32% YoY); **99% from subscriptions** — 3/6/12-month plans starting at ₹999 sold to *tenants and owners*. Plus 10–20% cuts on home loans, packers/movers, cleaning, interiors.

**The wedge.** **Killed the 1-month-rent broker commission** in a market where brokers charged tenants a full month's rent for a lease introduction. Sold subscription access directly to consumers instead. Became India's first proptech unicorn (2021, $1B).

**Trust.** In-person visit verification for listings; police-verification-as-a-service for tenants; owner-ID checked against property-tax receipts.

**Payments.** NoBrokerPay for rent (credit-card rent = points), NoBrokerHood for society management (~40k+ societies).

**Agent network.** Explicitly anti-agent. Owner ↔ tenant direct.

**Data moat.** Society-management footprint (NoBrokerHood) — captures every gated community in urban India.

**Copy for KE.** The **subscription-for-verified-leads model** ports beautifully. Nairobi tenants currently pay "viewing fees" (KES 500–2,000 per house shown) to informal brokers. A KES 500–1,000/month verified-listing subscription with in-person-visited houses is a direct upgrade.

**Don't copy.** "Zero broker" branding. Kenya has 50k agents who are the actual distribution channel — going to war with them is suicide, cooperate like Beike instead.

## 4. Zillow (US)

**Business model.** ~65% of revenue from **Premier Agent** — buyer's-agent lead sales via ZIP-code auction. Rest: rentals (Zillow Rental Manager), mortgage originations, new-construction ads.

**The wedge.** **Zestimate** — an AVM on every home in America. Median error 1.8–2.4% on-market, 7.0–7.2% off-market. Created the "I want to see my home's value" traffic hook.

**Trust.** Weak on listings; strong on estimates. The Zestimate became a *social object*.

**Payments.** Historically none. Zillow Offers (iBuyer) lost >$880M, shut Nov 2021.

**Data moat.** 160M+ property records × 20 years of on-portal price history.

**Copy for KE.** Build **a Kenyan AVM** — even a rough one on estate + bedrooms + finish level. Sell it back to banks (KCB, Equity) for mortgage-collateral valuation.

**Don't copy.** iBuying. In Kenya where title itself is disputed, this is a corporate suicide pact.

## 5. Rightmove (UK)

**Business model.** £425.1M revenue in 2025 (+9% YoY). **Estate agent subscriptions** — ARPA £1,530/mo in 2025.

**The wedge.** **Pure two-sided network effect run to completion.** ~75% share of UK online listings; 70–80% of consumer time on property portals.

**Trust.** Agent subscription itself acts as a filter — only registered UK estate agents can list.

**Payments.** None. Pure advertising portal.

**Data moat.** RBC estimates **~90% of Rightmove's data is proprietary and non-scrapeable** — the moat is behavioural signal (clicks, saves, contact intent) not visible listings.

**Copy for KE.** **Behavioural-signal telemetry from day one** — instrument every save, share, WhatsApp-forward, tour-request. That data is unscrapeable and is what makes the portal defensible even after Property24 has more listings.

**Don't copy.** The pure-portal ad-model as endgame. In KE at 1% agent registration penetration, listings-only monetisation will lose to any player that solves trust or payments.

## 6. Compass (US)

**Business model.** ~US$6.5B revenue pre-Anywhere, projected US$10B post-acquisition. Almost all revenue = agent commission split.

**The wedge.** **Tech-forward brokerage** — spent $1.6B building agent tooling (CRM, marketing automation, 3D tours, deal pipeline) and gave it away free to recruit top-producing agents.

**Data moat.** "Private Exclusives" — off-market listings visible only inside Compass.

**Copy for KE.** The **agent-tooling-as-recruiting-lever** playbook. Build a CRM + WhatsApp-integrated deal pipe + M-Pesa payout dashboard, and top Kenyan agents will pay to be on Vestra.

**Don't copy.** $1.6B tech spend + captive-brokerage model. Ship an 80/20 CRM in 3 months.

## 7. Doorstead + Belong (US)

**Doorstead** deprecated its standalone Rent Guarantee in 2025 and replaced it with **Place & Manage**: 5% mgmt fee, 21-day median lease-up.

**Belong** still offers **Guaranteed Rent**: 8% mgmt fee + 0.5-month placement, payment on the 1st. Or **Belong Upfront** — landlord takes full year's rent day-one at 16.99% mgmt fee.

**Copy for KE.** The **"Upfront" variant is genius for Kenya.** Offer "Vestra Advance" — 6–12 months rent paid on lease signing, financed against a rent-receivable pool, in exchange for 15–20% take-rate. Solves landlord's #1 objection to using any platform (cash timing).

**Don't copy.** Full US-style property management. Kenya has no equivalent legal infrastructure — subcontract to existing letting agents instead.

## 8. Property Finder + Bayut (UAE)

**The wedge.** Two verification products:
- **TruCheck™**: agent physically visits the property, opens Profolio app *within 500m of the address* (geofenced), uploads photos; QA team authenticates; badge expires and must be re-verified.
- **TruBroker™**: weekly leaderboard, TruPoints scoring, three badges ranking agents on search results.

**Copy for KE.** **The geofenced-physical-verification pattern is a direct import.** Vestra should require every listing to be photographed via the Vestra Capacitor app from within 100m of the geocoded property. This eliminates 80% of the fake-listings problem that plagues Property24 KE and Jiji.

## Kenya application — 5 wedges ranked by impact ÷ effort

**#1. Geofenced photo-verified listings (Bayut TruCheck clone).** *High impact, low effort.* Force every listing photo through the Vestra mobile app with GPS lock within 100m of the plot polygon. Badge expires every 30 days. 4-week build on Capacitor + Prisma.

**#2. M-Pesa rent escrow + auto-KRA remittance.** *Very high impact, medium effort.* Tenant pays Paybill → Vestra holds → pays landlord on the 1st → auto-remits the 7.5% MRI to KRA Paybill 222222 by the 20th. Landlords hate KRA compliance more than they hate agents; solve that and you own the rent rail. Vestra's QuintoAndar moment, minus the guarantee (yet).

**#3. Role-split agent commissions (Beike ACN clone).** *Very high impact, high effort.* Break every transaction into lister / tour-agent / closer / key-holder with scored payouts. Lets Vestra onboard the 49,500 non-EARB agents *without* falsely calling them licensed — they earn small roles while EARB-licensed agents anchor the closing role.

**#4. Landlord "Rent Advance" (Belong Upfront clone).** *High impact, medium effort.* Pay landlord 6 months rent upfront on signing, in exchange for a 15–20% mgmt fee, financed against the rent-receivable pool (Equity/KCB as debt partner). Do *not* offer an unpriced guarantee — QuintoAndar's QuintoCred shutdown is the cautionary tale.

**#5. Kenyan AVM ("Vestimate") + bank licensing.** *Medium impact, medium effort.* Build a rough neighbourhood-x-bedrooms-x-finish AVM. Sell to KCB/Equity/Absa for mortgage-collateral valuation (currently done manually at KES 15–30k per report). B2B revenue + top-of-funnel consumer hook. Zillow-style, without the iBuyer disaster.

**Sequencing.** Ship #1 + #2 in the first 90 days. Add #3 in months 3–9. #4 requires a debt facility so plan it for month 9–12. #5 is a 12-month data play. Instrument every user action Rightmove-style — the behavioural data moat is what makes Vestra defensible in year 3.
