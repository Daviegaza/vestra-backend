# Vestra Competitive Intelligence — Kenya + Africa Proptech Landscape

Date: 2026-07-04. Scope: 14 competitors, EA-first + cross-Africa comparables.

---

## 1. Property24 Kenya

**Position.** Owned by Naspers/Prosus via Property24 South Africa — the deepest-pocketed player in KE portal market. Public-facing claim: "Kenya's #1 property portal" with ~25,000 listings. App: 4.65★ / 640 ratings on Google Play — decent score masking serious quality issues.

**Monetization.** Monthly agent subscription starting around KSh 3,480/mo, featured-listing upsells. Legacy bundled discount with OLX (2017) proves they've historically had to buy adoption. No consumer-side monetization, no transaction take, no escrow, no landlord finance.

**Weak spots.** (1) Zero moderation — no report-listing feature in KE (unlike SA app), scammers list phantom units; (2) Stale inventory — houses dated 2020–22 still surfacing; (3) Slow listing approval taking weeks. Documented case: tenant flew to Nairobi against fake tenancy agreement issued via a Property24 listing.

**Cross-border.** Yes — Property24 also runs Nigeria; SA is HQ. KE inventory not integrated with SA diaspora search.

**Does well.** Programmatic SEO on suburb pages — they own top of Google for almost every Kenyan neighborhood × property-type combo.

**Threat.** Medium-high on brand recognition, low on execution quality. Verified-listing-only competitor with agent KYC would peel off high-intent buyers within 12 months.

## 2. BuyRentKenya

**Position.** Founded 2012 by Jamie Pujara; **acquired by Rushbox Digital Media Dec 3, 2025**. Transitional management under CEO Elizabeth Costabir, ~35 staff. Rushbox is not a proptech operator — expect either monetization tightening or product neglect during transition.

**Listings + monetization.** ~7,500 disclosed listings. Rate card 2024: Silver KSh 6,500/mo for 15 listings, Gold KSh 9,500/mo unlimited, tiers KSh 5,000–110,000. Credit-based "boost" upsells.

**Weak spots.** (1) Trustpilot flags "very bad support service"; (2) Punishing unit economics; (3) Rushbox acquisition = strategy vacuum; (4) No transactional layer.

**Cross-border.** No. Kenya-only.

**Does well.** Diaspora-targeted content marketing. SEO around "houses for rent in [suburb]".

**Threat.** Downgraded from High to Medium post-acquisition. **12–18 month window for a well-executed challenger.** This is Vestra's most stealable share.

## 3. HassConsult

**Position.** Est. 1992. Full-service consultancy — development, sales, letting, valuation, property management — skewed to middle/upper-market Nairobi. Small live portfolio (3 for sale, 16 for rent). They underwrite the pipeline, not classified volume.

**Data moat = the real asset.** The **Hass Composite / Hass Sales / Hass Rental Indices** are Kenya's only credible mix-adjusted longitudinal price indices. Coverage: **320+ suburbs/towns, back to Dec 2000, 199,700+ property observations**. Cited by media, KRA valuation debates, bank mortgage pricing, CBK financial-stability reports. **Single most valuable proprietary dataset in Kenyan proptech.**

**Weak spots.** Consulting-firm cost structure. Index published in PDFs, not queryable APIs. No consumer product. Zero mobile presence.

**Cross-border.** No.

**Threat.** Low as direct competitor, **high as data gatekeeper**. If Vestra generates transaction-verified price data, we can build a rival index in 24 months and license it to banks.

## 4. Knight Frank Kenya

**Position.** Global luxury brand. Kenyan office = HNW transaction advisory + research reports (Africa Report 2024/25, Prime Global Cities Index). Prime residential prices +6.17% YoY to Dec 2025; prime rentals +4.05%.

**Monetization.** Commission on high-ticket transactions. Consultancy retainers. Diaspora sales pipeline explicit growth vector.

**Weak spots.** Global-brand overhead makes them uncompetitive on transactions <USD 500k. Public inventory is a shop window. Diaspora capture depends on relationship-driven inbound, not funnel.

**Cross-border.** Yes — franchise network across EA + global buyer referrals.

**Does well.** Africa Report and prime residential index are marketing artifacts. **Vestra should replicate the format** (branded quarterly PDF + companion webpage) using our transaction data.

**Threat.** Low direct. Natural referral partner or acquisition target.

## 5. Cytonn Real Estate

**Position. Effectively defunct as going concern.** Court-ordered liquidation (2023), ~KSh 13.5–14bn owed to ~4,000 investors. Founder faces fraud charges. Remaining brand equity in **Cytonn Research reports** (weekly market notes, REIT tracker).

**REIT market truth.** 5 approved; only LAPTrust Imara I-REIT actively listed on main NSE; ILAM Fahari, Acorn I-REIT, Acorn D-REIT trade on Unquoted Securities Platform.

**Weak spots.** Brand toxic to retail investors.

**Threat.** Very low as competitor. **High as lesson** — Kenyan middle class burned by "guaranteed yield" structures. Vestra's escrow messaging MUST be structurally different: never promise yield, promise settlement guarantees.

## 6. Optiven

**Position.** Land-plot marketing machine. ~USD 5.7M revenue (2026), 37 employees. Value-added plots (fencing, security, roads, water, electricity) in Kitengela, Kiambu, Thika, Kajiado, Machakos, Nanyuki, Nyeri, Vipingo, Kangundo Rd, Kitale, Kisumu, Malindi.

**Playbook (worth studying).**
1. **Diaspora-first outbound** — dedicated Global Markets/Diaspora team, WhatsApp + Zoom close cycles.
2. **Campaign-driven sales** — "Black November Green Deals", Easter promos, referral tiering (paid after 20% deposit landed).
3. **Owned physical experience** — Karen + Kileleshwa offices + on-site tours.
4. **Content flywheel** — Wachiuri's YouTube + LinkedIn + weekly newsletter.
5. **Value-add packaging** — land + services bundle.

**Weak spots.** Own-inventory only. No secondary market. No rental exposure. Title-transfer timelines opaque.

**Does well.** Campaign cadence + diaspora sales team.

**Threat.** Medium in land segment.

## 7. Username Investments

**Position.** Optiven's direct rival. **21,000+ title deeds issued.** Plots from KSh 249,000. Won "Best Land and Investment Company" 26th Kenya Homes Expo.

**Model.** Direct-to-consumer land, seminar/expo funnel, **tiered referral commissions triggered after 20% buyer deposit** — aligned with revenue recognition. Online purchase + digital doc signing + online payments = more digitally native than Optiven.

**Does well.** **Online purchase flow for land.** Rare in KE. **Referral-commission-on-deposit** mechanic — clearest example of aligning incentives with actual cash conversion. Vestra's agent-trust program should mirror this: reputation points and commission unlock at verified deposit, not at listing view.

**Threat.** Medium in land segment, low elsewhere.

## 8. Estate Intel (Nigeria)

**Position.** Commercial real-estate **data platform**. Raised $500k pre-seed (2022). eiPro $100/yr consumer + custom reports floor $5,000. **120+ enterprise customers, 44+ cities including Nairobi.**

**Weakness in KE.** Kenya data thin and imported, not transaction-verified.

**Does well.** Productized data as SaaS with dashboards. Format Hass fails at.

**Threat.** Low as competitor, **relevant as benchmark** for the data product Vestra should build.

## 9. PropertyPro (Nigeria)

60,000+ listings. FDV owns 39%. Multi-country. Subscription tiers + sponsored placements. CEO admission: Nigerian classifieds market has struggled 8+ years to extract value from agents.

**Threat.** Very low in KE. Marginal presence.

## 10. Spleet (Nigeria)

**Position.** Started monthly-rent marketplace, pivoted to **embedded rent finance**. "Rent Now, Pay Later" — no-collateral loans up to ₦3M (~$6,000), ~3.5% monthly interest, payroll-linked, 1 month down + 11 months financed. Total raised $3.49M.

**Model to steal.** The **landlord-gets-annual-lump / tenant-pays-monthly split-flow** is the single most important product idea in African rental for Kenya's market — landlords want annual cash for KRA + upkeep, tenants can only afford monthly. Vestra should build this natively.

**Weak spots.** 3.5%/month ≈ 51% APR — regulatory + PR risk. Nigeria-only.

**Threat.** Zero direct today. **High if they enter Kenya.** Ship equivalent product first.

## 11. meQasa (Ghana)

40,000+ listings, 230+ developers, 2,500+ agents. Ghana #1. Revenue up 129% in 2018; ~AUD 500k H1 2022 at positive EBITDA. FDV portfolio.

**Does well.** **Pay-to-list gating that filters serious agents** — fewer scam listings than Property24 KE. Vestra should copy.

**Threat.** Not in KE. Instructive benchmark.

## 12. Property24 South Africa (parent)

Naspers/Prosus-owned. SA HQ dictates KE strategy — KE is satellite portal with minimal local investment. International expansion is stated corporate priority but capital allocation to KE thin.

**Threat.** **High latent, low active.** Assume 18–24 months of continued neglect. Vestra needs to entrench.

## 13. PayProp (South Africa)

**SaaS-for-landlords** rent management — automated collection, reconciliation, distribution, arrears management, audit logs. 2,500+ agency clients. UK, ZA, Canada. **No Kenya, no announced EA expansion.**

**Weakness.** Agency-focused (>10 units). Solo landlords — majority in Kenya — not served.

**Does well.** **Trust-account rent flow with auditability.** Vestra's escrow rails should study PayProp's reconciliation UX.

**Threat.** Zero today. **Preempt them by launching landlord-side rent automation + KRA MRI filing in the same tool.**

---

## 14. Lamudi / Jumia House (defunct)

**Died November 2017** — Rocket Internet dissolved AIG ahead of Jumia's NYSE IPO cleanup. Root causes: (1) No local moderation, spam overload; (2) "Market leadership at any cost" front-loaded burn; (3) Zero transactional layer; (4) Agents defected to WhatsApp + Facebook groups.

**Lesson for Vestra.** Portal-only economics don't work in African real estate. Survivors either shrank to profitability (meQasa) or got backed by strategic parents (Property24). A challenger must monetize the transaction, not the listing.

## Vestra's 3 Defensible Wedges

**Wedge 1 — Verified escrow rail tied to title-deed + KRA identity checks.**
Every incumbent monetizes listings; none monetize the transaction. Cytonn collapse + Property24 fake-tenancy scandals prove buyers have lost faith in trust-by-branding and will pay a settlement premium. Escrow — holding buyer funds until title transfer confirms at Ministry of Lands, or tenant deposit until landlord meets defined move-in condition — is a fee event on every deal. **Pricing: 1.5% of transaction value on sale, KSh 500 flat on rent deposits.** No incumbent can copy without redesigning their P&L — Property24 and BuyRentKenya boards will not fund it. **24-month lock-in window.**

**Wedge 2 — Native KRA MRI compliance embedded in landlord dashboard.**
KRA MRI (7.5% on gross monthly rent 280k–15M/yr, filed by 20th) plus 2026 migration to eRITS creates a compliance headache no portal has touched. Vestra collects rent via M-Pesa/bank, auto-computes 7.5%, files MRI return via eRITS API, produces compliance receipt in one flow. Landlords save accountant fee + eliminate 5% late-file penalty + 1%/month interest. **Retention weapon** — once landlord has 6 months rent history + filed returns inside Vestra, switching cost is punitive. HassConsult can't build this (consulting margins), Property24 won't (SA roadmap), Optiven/Username don't touch rental. **Single most defensible SaaS wedge in KE.**

**Wedge 3 — Agent trust graph with public reputation + commission-on-deposit unlocking.**
Every current portal treats agent verification as a checkbox. Vestra should treat it as the product. Borrow Username's referral-commission-on-deposit mechanic and generalize it: agents earn public reputation points only when a lead they source hits a **verified deposit-in-escrow milestone**. Fake listings, phantom viewings, ghost buyers stop paying out. Public agent profile with historical verified transaction count, dispute rate, average time-to-close, gated by Vestra's escrow data. Diaspora buyers — Knight Frank's segment — get a legible trust signal they've never had. Starves the fraud economy that lets Property24, Facebook groups, WhatsApp brokers coexist.

**Combined moat.** Escrow rails + KRA compliance + verified agent graph is a **regulated, data-network, reputation-gated stack**. Any single feature is copyable in 6 months. Three together copyable in **36+ months** — longer than any current KE competitor's runway and longer than Naspers's attention span for KE.
